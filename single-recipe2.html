Croissant

Indulge in the buttery, flaky goodness of homemade croissants! This recipe guides you through the steps of making the perfect croissant, perfect for breakfast or a delightful snack. Let’s create this classic pastry together!

Prep time

30 min.

Rise time

4 hours.

Servings

12 croissants

Tags : pastry breakfast baking

Instructions

Step 1

In a small bowl, combine 1 cup of warm milk, 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast, and 1 tablespoon sugar. Let it sit for 5-10 minutes until frothy.

Step 2

In a large mixing bowl, combine 4 cups all-purpose flour and 1 teaspoon salt. Add the yeast mixture, 1 tablespoon of melted butter, and mix until a dough forms.

Step 3

Knead the dough for about 10 minutes until smooth. Place in a greased bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise for 1 hour or until doubled in size.

Step 4

Roll the dough into a rectangle, then place 1 cup of chilled, softened butter in the center. Fold the dough over the butter, sealing the edges.

Step 5

Roll the dough into a large rectangle again, fold it into thirds, and repeat this process two more times. Let the dough rest in the fridge for 30 minutes after each folding.

Step 6

After the final fold, roll the dough into a rectangle about ¼ inch thick and cut into triangles. Roll each triangle from the base to the tip to form croissants.

Step 7

Place the croissants on a baking sheet, cover with a cloth, and let them rise for about 2 hours until puffy.

Step 8

Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Bake the croissants for 15-20 minutes or until golden brown.

Ingredients

1 cup warm milk

2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast

1 tablespoon sugar

4 cups all-purpose flour

1 teaspoon salt

1 tablespoon melted butter

1 cup chilled, softened butter (for laminating)

Tools

Mixing Bowls

Rolling Pin

Baking Sheet

Parchment Paper

Cling Film

Cooling Rack
